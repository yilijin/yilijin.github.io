Projects

  • A Large-Scale Dataset of Head and Gaze Behavior for 360° Videos and a Pilot Study (Advisor:Fangxin Wang)

    • I propose a novel quantitative taxonomy for 360° videos, which contains three technical metrics, camera motion, video quality, and dispersion of region of interest (ROI).

    • I collect a large-scale and high-diversity dataset with head and gaze behavior for 360° videos.

    • I do a pilot study on users' behaviors and get some interesting findings, such as (1) user’s head direction will follow his/her gaze direction with a most possible time interval of 0.12 seconds; and (2) users explore more horizontally than vertically.

    • I demonstrate a use case of our dataset in 360° video streaming scenario, and propose some other potential applications as well as future works.

  • Edge Assisted Multi-user 360° Video Streaming (Advisor:Fangxin Wang)

    • I for the first time propose a framework that jointly considers the user FoV prediction and video tile caching with edge computing toward QoE-oriented and economic 360° video streaming.

    • I propose a collaborative FoV prediction architecture that integrates the features of video content, user trajectory, and other users' records for combined prediction, which renders accurate and robust results.

    • I formulate the video tile caching problem that considers both the QoE maximization and cost minimization. I then solve it effectively with Lyapunov optimization and subgradient descent.

  • A DRL Approach for Verifying Minimum K-Link Failures (Advisor:Lizhao You and Qiao Xiang)

    • I formally define the minimum K-link failures configuration verification problem, and prove its strong NP-Hardness. Meanwhile, I also prove that finding two nodes' reachability is NP-Hard in configuration verification problem.

    • I propose an algorithm which consists of EC-DQN and Reachability Verifier: EC-DQN targets at finding min-cut edges incrementally and Reachability Verifier focuses on verifying the reachability of source and sink to determine whether EC-DQN should terminate.

    • Experiment results show the algorithm outperforms the state-of-the-art algorithm in terms of both efficiency and efficacy. For networks with 200 nodes, the algorithm runs 50 times faster and the approximation ratio is close to 1.

  • Effcient Network Control Plane Verification Algorithms for Data Center Networks (Advisor:Lizhao You)
